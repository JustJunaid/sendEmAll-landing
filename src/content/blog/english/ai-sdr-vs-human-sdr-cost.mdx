## The SDR cost question nobody answers honestly

You're choosing between three options for outbound sales development:

1. Hire a human SDR
2. Buy an AI SDR tool
3. Use an AI-assisted platform where software handles research and personalization, and a human handles strategy and replies

Everyone selling option 2 tells you option 1 is dead. Everyone selling option 1 tells you option 2 doesn't work. Neither is being straight with you.

Here's the honest breakdown — with real numbers.

## Human SDR: the fully loaded cost

When a VP of Sales budgets for one SDR, they rarely account for the true number. Here's what one human SDR actually costs per year in 2026:

| Cost Component | Low End | High End |
|----------------|---------|----------|
| Base salary | $45,000 | $65,000 |
| Commission / bonus | $20,000 | $30,000 |
| Benefits (health, 401k, PTO) | $12,000 | $18,000 |
| Payroll taxes | $7,000 | $9,000 |
| Tools (CRM, sequencer, data) | $5,000 | $10,000 |
| Laptop + equipment | $1,500 | $3,000 |
| Management overhead (10-15% of manager's time) | $12,000 | $20,000 |
| Training + onboarding | $3,000 | $5,000 |
| **Total Year 1** | **$105,500** | **$160,000** |

But Year 1 has a ramp problem. Most SDRs take 2-3 months to become productive. During ramp, you're paying full cost for partial output. If you factor in realistic quota attainment — 60% of SDRs miss quota according to The Bridge Group's annual survey — the *effective* cost per productive SDR climbs to $150,000-$300,000/year.

And if they quit after 14 months (the average tenure for SDRs), you start the cycle over.

### What you get for the money

A solid human SDR books 8-15 qualified meetings per month. They read replies with nuance. They know when a prospect's "not right now" means "try me in Q3" versus "never contact me again." They build relationships.

A great SDR is worth every dollar. The problem isn't the SDR — it's the economics. You need $500K+ in pipeline per SDR per year just to break even on their cost.

That's 5-25x cheaper than a human SDR. Sounds like a no-brainer. Until you look at the results.

### The conversion problem

AI SDRs have a throughput advantage — they can contact 10x the volume of a human. But conversion rates tell a different story.

| Metric | Human SDR | Full AI SDR | AI-Assisted |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Emails sent/day | 50-80 | 500-2,000 | 100-300 |
| Reply rate | 8-15% | 2-5% | 10-18% |
| Positive reply rate | 3-6% | 0.5-2% | 5-9% |
| Meeting book rate (from positive) | 60-80% | 20-40% | 50-70% |
| Meetings/month | 8-15 | 5-12 | 12-25 |
| Cost per meeting | $700-$1,500 | $150-$600 | $30-$100 |

Full AI SDRs generate volume. But their positive reply rates are significantly lower because they miss context. They send tone-deaf follow-ups. They misread industries. They sound like... AI.

The meetings they do book tend to be lower quality. Prospects who agreed to meet an AI agent often have lower purchase intent than those who responded to a thoughtful, personalized human touch.

### Where AI SDRs actually fail

After working with hundreds of outbound teams, here's what we see consistently:

**Nuanced replies.** A prospect writes, "We just signed a 2-year deal with your competitor, but I'm not thrilled with their support." A human SDR reads that as a warm lead to nurture. An AI SDR either ignores the signal or responds with a generic pitch.

**Industry context.** AI SDRs trained on general data don't understand that healthcare procurement cycles are 12-18 months, or that government buyers need FedRAMP compliance before anything else matters.

**Objection handling.** "We're not looking right now" has 15 different meanings depending on tone, context, and timing. AI SDRs have one playbook.

**Brand risk.** When an AI SDR sends something tone-deaf — and it will — your brand takes the hit. One bad email to a key account can close that door permanently.

## The third option: AI-assisted outbound

This is the model we built SendEmAll around, and it's also what the smartest sales teams are adopting independently.

The concept: AI handles the work humans are bad at (research, data processing, personalization at scale, infrastructure management). Humans handle the work AI is bad at (strategy, nuance, relationship building, reply handling).

### What AI does well

- **Signal monitoring.** Tracking job postings, funding announcements, tech stack changes, and leadership moves across thousands of companies simultaneously. No human can do this at scale.
- **Data enrichment.** Pulling firmographic, technographic, and contact data from multiple providers and merging it accurately.
- **First-draft personalization.** Writing email variations that reference specific company details, recent news, and relevant pain points.
- **Infrastructure management.** Handling domain rotation, Smart Ramp-Up schedules, DNS authentication, and sending optimization across dozens of mailboxes.

### What humans do well

- **Strategy.** Deciding which signals matter for your ICP. Choosing the right value prop for each segment.
- **Approval.** Reviewing AI-generated emails before they send. Catching the 10% that miss the mark.
- **Reply handling.** Reading between the lines. Knowing when to push, when to wait, when to escalate to an AE.
- **Relationship building.** Turning a cold reply into a warm conversation. That's still a deeply human skill.

## When each model makes sense

**Hire a human SDR when:**
- Your ACV is $50K+ and relationships matter
- You're selling into complex buying committees
- You have the budget and patience for 3-month ramp
- Your sales motion requires live demos and discovery calls as a first touch

**Use a full AI SDR when:**
- You're testing a new market and need fast volume
- Your product is self-serve with low ACV ($500-$5K)
- You have someone internally who can monitor and clean up AI mistakes daily
- You're OK with lower quality in exchange for higher quantity

**Use AI-assisted outbound when:**
- You want the volume of AI with the quality of human touch
- Your budget is $149-$599/month, not $5K-$13K/month
- You have 2-5 hours per week to review campaigns and handle replies
- You care about your brand reputation in your market
