## Clay changed the math

Clay is a remarkable product. The ability to build custom enrichment workflows, query 80+ data providers, and run AI agents on your data is unmatched. No other tool offers this level of flexibility.

But in early 2026, Clay raised prices significantly. The Explorer plan went from $149/mo (3,000 credits) to a pricing structure that burns credits faster for complex workflows. Teams that were comfortable at $149/mo found themselves needing the $349/mo Pro plan or higher to maintain the same volume.

For teams using Clay purely for basic enrichment (company data, contact info, email finding), the new pricing made alternatives more attractive. For teams using Clay's advanced features (multi-step waterfalls, AI research agents, custom logic), Clay remains hard to replace.

## What Clay does well (genuinely)

Let's be honest about Clay's strengths:

**80+ data providers in one interface.** Instead of signing up for ZoomInfo, Clearbit, Hunter, Dropcontact, and a dozen others, Clay queries them all through a single waterfall. If Provider A doesn't have the email, try Provider B, then C. This alone saves hundreds per month in separate subscriptions.

**Custom workflow logic.** If the company has raised funding in the last 6 months AND is hiring for sales roles AND uses Salesforce, enrich with these specific data points. This if/then logic is powerful and unique to Clay.

**AI research agent.** Clay can use AI to research prospects — read their LinkedIn, analyze their company, and generate custom data fields. For teams doing hyper-personalized outreach, this is genuinely valuable.

**Data flexibility.** Clay treats data as malleable. You can transform, combine, and restructure it in ways that spreadsheets and other tools can't match.

## What Clay doesn't do

**Send emails.** Clay is an enrichment tool, not a sending tool. You enrich in Clay, export to Instantly or Lemlist, then send from there. That's another subscription and another data handoff.

**Manage infrastructure.** Clay doesn't touch domains, mailboxes, DNS, or deliverability. That's your problem.

**Verify at scale.** Clay can query verification providers as part of a waterfall, but it costs credits. Basic verification through ZeroBounce at $15/mo is cheaper than burning Clay credits on verification.

**Discover leads.** Clay enriches records you already have. It can query databases to find contacts, but it's primarily an enrichment engine, not a discovery engine.

## When Clay is still the right choice

Stay with Clay if:

- **You need complex enrichment workflows.** Multi-step waterfalls with conditional logic, AI research, custom field generation — nothing else matches Clay for this.
- **You have technical team members** who can build and maintain Clay workflows. Clay's power comes with complexity.
- **Enrichment quality is your competitive advantage.** If hyper-personalized outreach based on deep prospect research is what differentiates your outreach, Clay's flexibility is worth the premium.
- **You already have the rest of the stack.** If you're happy with your sending platform, infrastructure, and verification — and you just need better enrichment — Clay is the best enrichment tool.

## When to switch

Consider alternatives if:

- **You use Clay for basic enrichment only.** Finding emails, getting company data, basic contact info. You don't need 80 providers and custom workflows for that. A simpler, cheaper tool handles it.
- **The 2026 pricing change pushed you past budget.** If your Clay bill doubled because of credit usage changes, evaluate whether the advanced features justify the new price.
- **You want to eliminate the Clay → Instantly → ZeroBounce handoff.** Every export/import between tools is a place where data gets lost, duplicated, or corrupted. One platform eliminates these handoffs.
- **You need the full stack, not just enrichment.** If you're paying Clay ($349) + Instantly ($97) + ZeroBounce ($15) + infrastructure ($120) = $581/mo, consolidating to one platform at $349/mo saves 40%.
